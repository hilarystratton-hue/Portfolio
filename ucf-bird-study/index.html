01 — Motivation

Why Study Bird Activity Throughout the Day?

Birds exhibit clear diurnal patterns — daily rhythms in behavior that vary with temperature, light, and resource availability. Understanding these rhythms is fundamental to ecology and wildlife management. This study was motivated by three core observations about avian behavior:

🌅 Diurnal Activity Patterns

Birds show diurnal activity: daily patterns in behavior that shift predictably across the hours of a day.

🌄 Morning Peak Activity

Many bird species are most active early in the morning, when temperatures are cooler and competition is high.

🎵 Morning-Linked Behaviors

Morning activity is linked to two key behaviors: foraging for food, and communication and territory defense.

🔬 Conservation Relevance

Understanding daily activity cycles is crucial for interpreting species-specific behavior and designing accurate wildlife surveys.

02 — Literature Context

Scientific Background

Prior ecological research has established a clear framework for how time of day influences bird detectability and behavior. This study situates itself within that framework.

🌞 Peak Richness at Sunrise

Bird species richness usually peaks shortly after sunrise, when calling rates and foraging activity are highest.

🌡️ Afternoon Decline

Activity decreases later in the day as temperatures rise and birds reduce their energetic expenditure.

🌿 Environmental Influence

Environmental conditions — including wind, precipitation, and ambient noise — strongly influence bird detectability in the field.

🎶 Dawn Chorus Effect

Birds are easier to detect in the early morning because they are more active and make more sounds, a phenomenon known as the dawn chorus.

03 — Research Design

Objective & Hypothesis

The research team established a clear objective and formulated a testable hypothesis prior to data collection.

Objective

To determine how bird species richness and abundance changes throughout the day at the UCF Arboretum.

Hypothesis

Bird species richness and abundance will increase earlier in the morning and decrease later in the day due to peak morning activity.

Prediction

The highest number of bird species will be observed in the early morning sampling times.

04 — Field Site

Study Location: UCF Arboretum

University of Central Florida Arboretum — Orlando, Florida

The study was conducted at the University of Central Florida (UCF) Arboretum, a diverse ecological site containing multiple habitat types. The Arboretum's variety of environments — lakeside areas, wooded regions, and open spaces — made it an ideal setting for comparing bird activity patterns across different microhabitats within a single, accessible site.

🏞️ Habitat Diversity

The Arboretum contains a variety of habitats, including areas near a lake, wooded regions, and open spaces.

📍 Sampling Points

Research was carried out at specific sampling points located throughout the Arboretum to allow comparison across different environmental areas.

🌲 Natural Setting

The site provided a natural setting suitable for observing local bird species in conditions representative of the broader Florida urban-adjacent ecosystem.

05 — Methodology

Methods

Field Sampling Design

📋 Point Count Survey

A Point Count Survey method was used for field sampling — a standard and widely validated approach for measuring bird species richness and abundance.

🧍 Stationary Observers

Observers stood at fixed locations to record birds. At each location, observers remained stationary and birds were recorded for a fixed time period of 7 minutes.

👁️ Identification Method

All birds were identified by visual observation and vocalizations (calls and songs).

📱 Digital Surveys

Surveys were conducted using a Google Form at each point to standardize data entry across all observers and sampling events.

Bird Identification Methods

👁️ Visual & Audio

Birds were identified using both visual characteristics and sound. This dual approach improved accuracy when birds were obscured by foliage.

📱 iNaturalist

iNaturalist was used as a digital identification tool, enabling community-validated species identification from photographs.

🎵 Merlin Bird ID

Merlin Bird ID app was used for audio identification, with bird calls played through the app to confirm species from vocalizations.

Sampling Method — Pros & Cons

✅ Advantages

  • Easy and quick method to implement
  • Good for measuring species richness and abundance
  • Consistent 7-minute count window ensures comparability
  • Well-suited for comparing morning vs. afternoon activity patterns

⚠️ Limitations

  • Some birds may be missed during the count window
  • Weather and background noise can affect detection results
  • Results depend on observer identification skill
  • Birds may move between or during counts

07 — Interpretation

Discussion: Interpretation of Results

Summary Finding

These results do not support the hypothesis that bird species richness would be highest in the morning, but do support the finding that abundance is highest in the early morning to mid-morning, due to increased activity during that time.

Why Species Richness Was Consistent

🏠 Habitat Fidelity

Certain species remain in their habitat throughout the day, so they can be detected at any time.

📍 Fixed Locations

Sampling the same locations repeatedly means the same species pool is available at all time periods.

👁️ Single Detection Threshold

Even if a bird is less active, it only needs to be seen once to be recorded, regardless of the hour.

📅 Short Study Window

Sampling within a single week limits species turnover, maintaining a stable species pool throughout.

Why Abundance Was Higher in the Morning

🍽️ Peak Feeding Activity

Morning represents the primary foraging window for most bird species, driving higher bird counts.

🎶 Vocalization Rate

Birds are easier to detect when they are calling in the morning, inflating apparent abundance numbers.

🌡️ Cooler Temperatures

Lower morning temperatures make birds more active, increasing movement and visibility to observers.

08 — Literature Comparison

Comparison to Scientific Literature

The team's findings were compared to existing ecological research on diurnal bird activity patterns.

Previous studies have shown that bird activity and detectability are often highest in the early morning due to increased vocalization during the Dawn Chorus — the period when birds are vocalizing most intensively.

📄 Nadeau et al. (2008)

C. P. Nadeau et al. (2008) found that probability of detecting wetland-dependent birds in Florida was significantly higher during morning point-count surveys compared to later in the day.

🔄 Diverging Results

The differing results from this study suggest more complex factors are influencing bird activity at the UCF Arboretum site.

🔬 Possible Explanations

The divergence may be due to variation in detection probability, environmental conditions specific to the site, or the limited sampling window of one week.

09 — Applied Impact

How This Research Helps Arboretum Management

Shows the best time of day to observe birds, enabling Arboretum staff to schedule more effective wildlife surveys.

✅ Survey Accuracy

Helps make bird surveys more accurate by identifying which time windows maximize bird detection rates.

🌿 Habitat Planning

📈 Long-term Monitoring

Supports long-term monitoring of birds in the Arboretum by establishing a baseline dataset for future comparison.

10 — Critical Evaluation

Limitations of the Study

The research team identified several methodological limitations that should be considered when interpreting the results:

🌤️ Weather Variability

Weather differences between the different days and times of sampling could have influenced bird behavior and detectability.

📅 Limited Observation Days

A limited number of observation days restricted the statistical power and temporal scope of the dataset.

🔊 Noise Interference

Background noise, including talking among observers, could have affected bird detection and disturbed the study subjects.

👥 Observer Variation

Different observer skills among student participants may have introduced inconsistencies in identification accuracy.

🐦 Bird Movement

Birds may have moved during the count window, potentially leading to double-counting or missed detections.

🗺️ Spatial Coverage

Limited spatial coverage within the Arboretum means some habitats and their bird communities may not be fully represented.

Data entry issues were also noted as an additional source of potential error in the final dataset.

11 — Looking Ahead

Future Research Directions

🌇 Expanded Time Windows

Survey birds at more times of day, including evening surveys, to capture a more complete picture of daily activity cycles.

📆 Extended Data Collection

Collect data on more days to make results more statistically reliable and reduce the influence of day-to-day weather variation.

🍂 Seasonal Comparisons

Study bird species changes across different seasons to capture migratory patterns and seasonal habitat use in Florida.
